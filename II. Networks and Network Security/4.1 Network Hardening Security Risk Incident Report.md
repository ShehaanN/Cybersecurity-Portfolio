# Security Risk Assessment Report: Network Hardening

## Part 1: Select up to Three Hardening Tools and Methods to Implement

**1. Implementing Multi-factor Authentication (MFA)**
* MFA requires users to verify their identity using multiple factors before accessing systems or applications. These factors may include something you know (password or PIN), something you have (ID cards), or something you are (biometrics such as fingerprint or facial recognition).

**2. Enforcing Strong Password Policies**
* Implement strict password requirements that enforce complexity (letters, numbers, and symbols), discourage password sharing, and reduce the risk of weak credentials. Additionally, account lockout policies can be applied after a limited number of failed login attempts to help prevent brute force attacks.

**3. Performing Firewall Maintenance Regularly and Port Filtering**
* Regular firewall maintenance ensures security rules are up to date and capable of detecting and blocking malicious traffic. Port filtering should also be applied to restrict unnecessary open ports and limit network exposure to potential attacks.

## Part 2: An Explanation of my Recommendations

1. Implement Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)
	- MFA strengthens authentication security by adding an extra verification layer beyond passwords. This significantly reduces the risk of unauthorized access, even if credentials are compromised through brute force or other related attacks.

2. Enforce Strong Password Policies
	- Strong password enforcement reduces the likelihood of attackers successfully guessing or cracking passwords. It improves overall account security and helps prevent unauthorized access to critical systems. 

3. Configure Firewalls with Regular Maintenance and Port Filtering
	- Properly configured firewalls monitor and control incoming and outgoing traffic, helping to block suspicious activity and unauthorized access attempts. Port filtering further reduces the attack surface by ensuring only necessary services are exposed, helping protect against scanning, exploitation, and DDoS attacks.

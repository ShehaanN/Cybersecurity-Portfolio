# NIST Cybersecurity Framework Analysis Report

### Summary

The organization experienced a Distributed Denial of Service (DDoS) attack caused by a flood of ICMP packets targeting the internal network. As a result, network services became unavailable for approximately two hours. The cybersecurity team identified that the attack was made possible through an improperly configured firewall, which allowed malicious ICMP traffic into the network. The team responded by blocking incoming ICMP packets, temporarily disabling non-critical services, and restoring critical systems to resume business operations.

---

## NIST CSF

|No|Description|
|:-:|---|
|**1**|**Identify:** The cybersecurity team identified that a malicious actor exploited an improperly configured firewall to launch an ICMP flood attack against the organization’s internal network. This attack overwhelmed network resources and disrupted critical services. Security assessments and audits revealed that the firewall lacked proper traffic filtering and rate-limiting rules, creating a vulnerability that enabled the attack.|
|**2**|**Protect:** To strengthen network security, the organization implemented new firewall rules to limit incoming ICMP traffic and enabled source IP verification to reduce spoofed packets. Additionally, the organization deployed an IDS/IPS solution to identify and filter suspicious network traffic. These protective measures help reduce the likelihood of future DDoS attacks and improve overall network resilience.|
|**3**|**Detect:** The organization deployed network monitoring tools to continuously monitor traffic patterns and detect unusual activity in real time. Monitoring systems and IDS/IPS technologies were configured to alert the security team when abnormal ICMP traffic or suspicious behavior is detected, improving the speed and effectiveness of threat detection.|
|**4**|**Respond:** During the incident, the cybersecurity team responded by blocking incoming ICMP packets, isolating affected services, and temporarily shutting down non-critical systems to stabilize the network. Critical services were restored as quickly as possible to minimize operational disruption. The team also analyzed network logs and traffic captures to determine the source and nature of the attack and implemented improved response procedures for future incidents.|
|**5**|**Recover:** Recovery efforts focused on restoring affected systems and network services to normal operation after the attack was contained. Critical services were prioritized to minimize downtime and business impact. The organization also reviewed and improved recovery procedures to ensure faster restoration and improved resilience against future cybersecurity incidents.|
|**Reflections/Notes**|This incident demonstrates the importance of properly configured firewalls, continuous network monitoring, and proactive security controls in defending against DDoS attacks. Regular security assessments, monitoring systems, and incident response planning are essential for improving organizational resilience and protecting critical network infrastructure.|
